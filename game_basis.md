# Gladiator Deckbuilder - Game Design Specification

## Overview

This game is a Roman-themed deckbuilding game inspired heavily by *Into the Echoside*.

Players compete to earn the most **Victory Points (VP)** by improving their deck, purchasing powerful cards, completing Arena Challenges, and leveraging faction synergies.

The game is designed for:

* Physical tabletop play
* Web
* iOS
* Android

Maximum Players: **6**

Typical Winning Score: **30-40 VP**

---

# Core Resources

## Coins

Coins are the primary resource used to purchase cards.

Players generate Coin through card effects.

Unused Coin is lost at the end of the turn.

---

## Victory Points (VP)

Victory Points determine the winner of the game.

VP is earned through:

* Cards in deck
* Arena victories
* Epics
* Certain card effects

Highest VP at game end wins.

---

## Valor

Valor is used only for Arena Challenges.

Valor is generated by cards.

Players commit cards with Valor to attempt Arena Challenges.

---

# Card Types

## Faction Cards

Faction cards make up the majority of the Gallery deck.

There are three factions:

### Ludus

Theme:

* Gladiators
* Arena fighters
* Trainers
* Arena personnel

Identity:

* Draw cards
* Deck refinement
* Trashing
* Gallery manipulation
* Highest average Valor
* Arena focused

Banding Bonus:

When 3 Ludus cards are played during a turn:

> Draw 1 card

---

### Senate

Theme:

* Politicians
* Magistrates
* Consuls
* Orators
* Nobility

Identity:

* Control
* Manipulation
* Sabotage
* Recursion
* Item interaction
* Gallery manipulation

Banding Bonus:

When 3 Senate cards are played during a turn:

> Gain +2 Coin

---

### Legion

Theme:

* Soldiers
* Officers
* Scouts
* Standard Bearers
* Military Command

Identity:

* Flexible effects
* Copying
* Imperial Favor generation
* Augury
* Utility
* Moderate Valor

Banding Bonus:

When 3 Legion cards are played during a turn:

> Gain 1 Flavor card

---

# Item Cards

Items are placed into play.

Items remain in play until:

* Discarded
* Destroyed
* Removed by effects

Items provide ongoing benefits.

Examples:

* Weapons
* Armor
* Relics
* Military Standards
* Political Decrees

Item design will be finalized later.

---

# Event Cards

When an Event enters the Gallery:

1. Resolve the Event immediately.
2. Discard the Event.
3. Replace it with a new Gallery card.

Events never enter player decks.

Current Event List:

* Bread and Circuses
* Senate Decree
* Barbarian Incursion
* Plague Spreads
* Triumph of Rome
* Grain Shortage
* Temple Omen

Additional Events may be added later.

---

# Flavor Cards

Flavor cards are small one-time effects.

When drawn:

1. Resolve immediately.
2. Discard after use.

Flavor cards do not remain in decks.

Examples:

* Draw cards
* Gain Coins
* Destroy cards
* Recover cards
* Temporary bonuses

---

# Epic Cards

Epic cards are powerful, expensive cards.

Epic cards:

* Do not belong to a faction
* Have stronger effects
* Usually provide high VP

Three Epics are visible at all times.

When purchased:

* Replace with next Epic

Examples:

* Spartacus
* Crixus
* Julius Caesar
* Pompey
* Scipio Africanus
* Mark Antony
* Constantine
* Oenomaus

Current Epic Pool Size:

15 cards

---

# Gallery

The Gallery is the main purchasing row.

Contains:

* Faction cards
* Items
* Events

Visible Cards:

6

When a card leaves:

* Immediately replace it

Events trigger and replace themselves automatically.

# Arena System

Arena replaces the Fiend system from Into the Echoside.

Arena Challenges are optional.

Players are never required to challenge the Arena.

---

## Arena Layout

Visible:

* Arena Deck
* Current Arena Challenge

Only the top Arena Challenge is active.

---

## Arena Attempt

During the Arena Phase:

A player may choose to challenge the Arena.

The challenger:

1. Commits up to 3 cards face down.
2. Other players may respond.

---

## Support / Sabotage

Each opponent may play:

* 1 Support card
* 1 Sabotage card

Responses are played face down.

After all responses:

1. Reveal committed cards.
2. Reveal support cards.
3. Reveal sabotage cards.

Resolve totals.

---

## Arena Resolution

Calculate:

Challenge Valor

* Support

- Sabotage

Compare against Arena Requirement.

### Success

Player receives Arena reward.

Arena card is discarded.

Reveal next Arena Challenge.

### Failure

Player gains:

## Crowd Disfavor

---

# Crowd Disfavor

Crowd Disfavor is a negative card.

Added to player's discard pile when they fail an Arena Challenge.

Properties:

* 0 Valor
* -1 VP

Can be removed by card effects.

Crowd Disfavor deck is unlimited for practical purposes.

---

# Arena Rewards

Arena cards provide:

* VP
* Other rewards
* Special effects

Arena victories are intended to be meaningful but not mandatory.

Ludus should naturally perform best at Arena.

Senate should be strongest at sabotage.

Legion should be strongest at support and recovery.

* Dominion
* Ascension
